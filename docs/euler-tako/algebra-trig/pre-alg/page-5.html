Factoring Polynomials

Factoring is a method applied to polynomials which allows us to simplify them. This often can make manipulating and analyzing these expressions vastly easier.

Greatest Common Factor​

The greatest common factor (GCF) of polynomials is the largest polynomial that divides evenly into the polynomials.

To find the GCF of an expression, we first identify the GCF of the coefficients and then of the variables. Once we find the GCFs, we combine them to get the GCF of the expression. For example, the GCF of 121212 and 202020 is 444 and the GCF of x2x^2x2 and xxx is xxx. Using these facts, we can conclude that the GCF of 12x212x^212x2 and 20x20x20x is 4x4x4x.

We can factor using the GCF by dividing each term in the polynomial by the GCF. The factored expression is the product of the GCF and the expression obtained by dividing. For example, the polynomial 12x2+20x12x^2 + 20x12x2+20x has as GCF of 4x4x4x. This means the factored expression is 4x(3x+5)4x(3x + 5)4x(3x+5).

Factoring Trinomials​

A trinomial of the form x2+bx+cx^2 + bx + cx2+bx+c (has a leading coefficient of 111) can be written in factored form as (x+p)(x+q)(x + p)(x + q)(x+p)(x+q) where pq=cpq = cpq=c and p+q=bp + q = bp+q=b.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

x2−3x−10x^2 - 3x - 10x2−3x−10

Let p=2p = 2p=2 and q=−5q = -5q=−5, then pq=−10pq = -10pq=−10 and p+q=−3p + q = -3p+q=−3.

(x+p)(x+q)=(x+2)(x−5)(x + p)(x + q) = (x + 2)(x - 5)(x+p)(x+q)=(x+2)(x−5)

note

Some polynomials cannot be factored. These polynomials are considered to be prime.

Factor by Grouping​

To factor a trinomial in the form ax2+bx+cax^2 + bx + cax2+bx+c by grouping, we need to find two numbers with a product of acacac and a sum of bbb. We can use these numbers to seperate bxbxbx into a sum of two terms and can factor each portion of the binomial seperately using GCF.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

7x2+48x−77x^2 + 48x - 77x2+48x−7 where a=7a = 7a=7, b=48b = 48b=48, c=−7c = -7c=−7

ac=−49=49×−1ac = -49 = 49 \times -1ac=−49=49×−1 and b=48=49−1b = 48 = 49 - 1b=48=49−1

7x2+49x−x−77x^2 + 49x - x - 77x2+49x−x−7

7x(x+7)−1(x+7)7x(x + 7) - 1(x + 7)7x(x+7)−1(x+7)

Both expressions have a common GCF of (x+7)(x + 7)(x+7). There will always be a common GCF like this.

(7x−1)(x+7)(7x - 1)(x + 7)(7x−1)(x+7)

Exponents​

Even fractional or negative exponents can be factored by pulling out the GCF and factoring with it.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

3x(x+2)−13+4(x+2)233x(x + 2)^{\frac{-1}{3}} + 4(x + 2)^{\frac{2}{3}}3x(x+2)3−1​+4(x+2)32​

The GCF is (x+2)−13(x + 2)^{-\frac{1}{3}}(x+2)−31​.

3x(x+2)−133x(x + 2)^{-\frac{1}{3}}3x(x+2)−31​ divided by the GCF is 3x3x3x and 4(x+2)234(x + 2)^{\frac{2}{3}}4(x+2)32​ divided by the GCF is 4(x+2)4(x + 2)4(x+2).

Factoring this gives us (x+2)−13(3x+4(x+2))(x + 2)^{-\frac{1}{3}}(3x + 4(x + 2))(x+2)−31​(3x+4(x+2)).

Finally, simplifying gives us (x+2)−13(7x+8)(x + 2)^{-\frac{1}{3}}(7x + 8)(x+2)−31​(7x+8).

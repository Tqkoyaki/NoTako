Complex Numbers

Complex numbers are numbers that consist of a real part and an imaginary part often in the form of a+bia + bia+bi where aaa is the real part, bbb is the coefficient of the imaginary part, and iii is the imaginary unit which is defined as i=−1i = \sqrt{-1}i=−1​.

We can combine our knowledge of trigonometry and complex numbers together to express complex numbers in polar form and perform operations such as finding products, quotients, powers, and roots of complex numbers. This gives us a new perspective on complex numbers and allows us to solve problems that would be difficult or impossible to solve using only rectangular form.

Graphing Complex Numbers​

Plotting complex numbers of the form a+bia + bia+bi on a coordinate plane is similar to plotting points in a Cartesian coordinate system with the exception that the horizontal axis represents the real part aaa and the vertical axis represents the imaginary part bibibi.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

Plot the complex number 1+5i1 + 5i1+5i on the complex plane.

From the origin, we move 1 unit to the right because a=1a = 1a=1 and then move 5 units up because b=5b = 5b=5. The point (1,5)(1, 5)(1,5) represents the complex number 1+5i1 + 5i1+5i on the complex plane.

Absolute Value​

The absolute value of a complex number a+bia + bia+bi is the same as the magnitude or ∣z∣|z|∣z∣. This means that the absolute value of a complex number is the measure of the distance from the origin to the point (a,b)(a, b)(a,b) on the complex plane. So, Given z=a+biz = a + biz=a+bi, a complex number, the absolute value of zzz is defined as ∣z∣=aa2a2+b2|z| = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^{a^2}} a^2 + b^2}∣z∣=aa2a2+b2​.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

Find the absolute value of the complex number z=12−5iz = 12 - 5iz=12−5i.

To find the absolute value of zzz, we use the formula ∣z∣=aa2a2+b2|z| = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^{a^2}} a^2 + b^2}∣z∣=aa2a2+b2​ where a=12a = 12a=12 and b=−5b = -5b=−5.

∣z∣=aa2122+(−5)2=a2144+25=a2169=13|z| = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^{a^2}} 12^2 + (-5)^2} = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^2} 144 + 25} = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^2} 169} = 13∣z∣=aa2122+(−5)2​=a2144+25​=a2169​=13

So, the absolute value of the complex number z=12−5iz = 12 - 5iz=12−5i is 131313.

Complex Numbers in Polar Form​

The polar form of a complex number is another way to express complex numbers using trigonometric functions. A complex number in polar form expresses a number in terms of an angle θ\thetaθ and its distance from the origin rrr.

Considering the complex number z=x+yiz = x + yiz=x+yi is expressed in rectangular form, we can use the same trigonometric relationships to convert it to polar form. Thse identities are...

  1. x=rcos⁡(θ)x = r \cos(\theta)x=rcos(θ)
  2. y=rsin⁡(θ)y = r \sin(\theta)y=rsin(θ)
  3. r=aa2x2+y2r = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^{a^2}} x^2 + y^2}r=aa2x2+y2​

We can substitute these identities into the rectangular form of a complex number, z=x+yiz = x + yiz=x+yi, to get the following conversion...

z=x+yi=rcos⁡θ+rsin⁡θ(i)=r(cos⁡θ+isin⁡θ)\begin{array}{ccccccccccccc} z &=& x + yi \\[1em] &=& r \cos \theta + r \sin \theta (i) \\[1em] &=& r (\cos\theta + i \sin \theta) \end{array}z​===​x+yircosθ+rsinθ(i)r(cosθ+isinθ)​

The polar form is written as z=r(cos⁡θ+isin⁡θ)z = r (\cos\theta + i \sin \theta)z=r(cosθ+isinθ) where rrr is the modulus (or absolute value) of the complex number and θ\thetaθ is the argument (or angle) of the complex number.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

Express z=3+iz = \sqrt{3} + iz=3​+i in polar form.

We can find the modulus rrr first using the formula r=aa2x2+y2r = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^{a^2}} x^2 + y^2}r=aa2x2+y2​ where x=3x = \sqrt{3}x=3​ and y=1y = 1y=1.

r=aa2(3)2+12=a23+1=a24=2r = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^{a^2}} (\sqrt{3})^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^2} 3 + 1} = \sqrt{\vphantom{a^2} 4} = 2r=aa2(3​)2+12​=a23+1​=a24​=2

Next, we can find the argument θ\thetaθ using either cos⁡θ=xr\cos \theta = \frac{x}{r}cosθ=rx​ or sin⁡θ=yr\sin \theta = \frac{y}{r}sinθ=ry​. We will use cos⁡θ=xr\cos \theta = \frac{x}{r}cosθ=rx​...

cos⁡θ=xr=32→θ=π6\cos \theta = \dfrac{x}{r} = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \rightarrow \theta = \dfrac{\pi}{6}cosθ=rx​=23​​→θ=6π​

Now that we have both rrr and θ\thetaθ, we can substitute them into the polar form equation z=r(cos⁡θ+isin⁡θ)z = r (\cos\theta + i \sin \theta)z=r(cosθ+isinθ).

So, z=3+iz = \sqrt{3} + iz=3​+i in polar form is z=2(cos⁡(π6)+isin⁡(π6))z = 2 \left( \cos \left(\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) + i \sin \left(\dfrac{\pi}{6}\right) \right)z=2(cos(6π​)+isin(6π​)).

Finding Products​

Now that we can convert between rectangular and polar forms of complex numbers, we can use the polar form to apply various operations on complex numbers. The first operation we will look at is finding the product of two complex numbers in polar form.

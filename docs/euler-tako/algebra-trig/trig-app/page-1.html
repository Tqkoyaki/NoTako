Law of Sines

So far we have derived various tools like the Pythagorean Theorem and Trigonometric Identities to solve problems involving right triangles. Now we will extend our knowledge by deriving tools like the Law of Sines in order to solve problems involving all types of triangles.

Oblique Triangles​

Any triangle that is not a right triangle is an oblique triangle. We can draw a perpendicular line from one vertex to the opposite side for any of these oblique triangles which will form two right triangles. We can then use all the properties of right triangles to solve the oblique triangle (finding all the missing sides and angles).

This method will work for any oblique triangle, but it is not always the most efficient method. Let's look at three situations for which we will derive a more efficient method to solve...

  1. ASA (Angle-Side-Angle) - The measurements of two angles and a side between them are known.
  1. AAS (Angle-Angle-Side) - The measurements of two angles and a side opposite one of them are known.
  1. SSA (Side-Side-Angle) - The measurements of two sides and an angle not between them are known.
note

We can solve any oblique triangle as long as we have at least three known values (sides or angles) where at least one of them is a side.

Law of Sines​

We can derive the Law of Sines by drawing a perpendicular line from one vertex to the opposite side of an oblique triangle and then using the properties of right triangles. Let aaa, bbb, and ccc be the lengths of the sides of an oblique triangle and let α\alphaα, β\betaβ, and γ\gammaγ be the angles opposite those sides. We can draw a perpendicular line from β\betaβ to side bbb where the length of the perpendicular line is hhh. This gives us the following triangle...

Using the right triangle properties, we know that sin⁡α=hc\sin \alpha = \dfrac{h}{c}sinα=ch​ and sin⁡γ=ha\sin \gamma = \dfrac{h}{a}sinγ=ah​. We can then solve for hhh for both equations to get h=csin⁡αh = c \sin \alphah=csinα and h=asin⁡γh = a \sin \gammah=asinγ. Finally, setting these two equations equal and simplifying gives us...

csin⁡α=asin⁡γ(1ac)(csin⁡α)=(1ac)(asin⁡γ)sin⁡αa=sin⁡γc\begin{array}{cccccc} c \sin \alpha &=& a \sin \gamma \\[0.5em] (\dfrac{1}{ac}) (c \sin \alpha) &=& (\dfrac{1}{ac}) (a \sin \gamma) \\[1em] \dfrac{\sin \alpha}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin \gamma}{c} \\[1em] \end{array}csinα(ac1​)(csinα)asinα​​===​asinγ(ac1​)(asinγ)csinγ​​

Now instead of drawing a perpendicular line from β\betaβ to side bbb, we can draw a perpendicular line from α\alphaα to side aaa or from γ\gammaγ to side ccc. We can use the same process to obtain...

sin⁡αa=sin⁡βb&sin⁡βb=sin⁡γc\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \dfrac{\sin \alpha}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin \beta}{b} & \& & \dfrac{\sin \beta}{b} &=& \dfrac{\sin \gamma}{c} \end{array}asinα​​=​bsinβ​​&​bsinβ​​=​csinγ​​

Putting these equations together gives us the Law of Sines...

sin⁡αa=sin⁡βb=sin⁡γc\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \dfrac{\sin \alpha}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin \beta}{b} &=& \dfrac{\sin \gamma}{c} \end{array}asinα​​=​bsinβ​​=​csinγ​​

This is an ASA triangle because we know two angles and a side which is between them. This means we can use the Law of Sines to find the missing side and angle.

Before we use the Law of Sines, we can easily find the missing angle β\betaβ by using the fact that the sum of the angles in a triangle is 180∘180^\circ180∘.

α+β+γ=180∘98∘+β+43∘=180∘β+141∘=180∘β=39∘\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \alpha + \beta + \gamma &=& 180^\circ \\[0.5em] 98^\circ + \beta + 43^\circ &=& 180^\circ \\[0.5em] \beta + 141^\circ &=& 180^\circ \\[0.5em] \beta &=& 39^\circ \end{array}α+β+γ98∘+β+43∘β+141∘β​====​180∘180∘180∘39∘​

Now that we know all the angles, we can start finding the values of the missing sides. We will do this by using the known value of bbb and the Law of Sines.

sin⁡αa=sin⁡βbsin⁡γc=sin⁡βbsin⁡98∘a=sin⁡39∘22sin⁡43∘c=sin⁡39∘2222sin⁡98∘sin⁡39∘=a22sin⁡43∘sin⁡39∘=c34.6≈a23.8≈c\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \dfrac{\sin \alpha}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin \beta}{b} &&&& \dfrac{\sin \gamma}{c} &=& \dfrac{\sin \beta}{b} \\[1.5em] \dfrac{\sin 98^\circ}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin 39^\circ}{22} &&&& \dfrac{\sin 43^\circ}{c} &=& \dfrac{\sin 39^\circ}{22} \\[1.5em] \dfrac{22 \sin 98^\circ}{\sin 39^\circ} &=& a &&&& \dfrac{22 \sin 43^\circ}{\sin 39^\circ} &=& c \\[1.5em] 34.6 &\approx& a &&&& 23.8 &\approx& c \end{array}asinα​asin98∘​sin39∘22sin98∘​34.6​===≈​bsinβ​22sin39∘​aa​​​​csinγ​csin43∘​sin39∘22sin43∘​23.8​===≈​bsinβ​22sin39∘​cc​

note

We can also invert the Law of Sines to get the following equation...

asin⁡α=bsin⁡β=csin⁡γ\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \dfrac{a}{\sin \alpha} &=& \dfrac{b}{\sin \beta} &=& \dfrac{c}{\sin \gamma} \end{array}sinαa​​=​sinβb​​=​sinγc​​

Both equations represent the same relationship between the sides and angles of a triangle thus both are variations of the Law of Sines.

β≈180∘−48.3∘β≈131.7∘\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \beta &\approx& 180^\circ - 48.3^\circ \\[1.5em] \beta &\approx& 131.7^\circ \end{array}ββ​≈≈​180∘−48.3∘131.7∘​

Finding Area​

The area of a triangle can be found using the formula A=12bhA = \dfrac{1}{2}bhA=21​bh where bbb is the length of the base and hhh is the height (the perpendicular line from the opposite vertex to the base). However, to be able to use this formula, we need to know the value of hhh. Luckily, we can remedy this issue for oblique triangles by rewriting the formula using the Law of Sines.

In both figures above, we can draw a perpendicular line to represent the height and then apply the trigonometric property sin⁡α=oppositehypotenuse=hc\sin \alpha = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \dfrac{h}{c}sinα=hypotenuseopposite​=ch​. After some rearranging, we can solve for hhh:

sin⁡α=hch=csin⁡α\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \sin \alpha &=& \dfrac{h}{c} \\[1.5em] h &=& c \sin \alpha \end{array}sinαh​==​ch​csinα​

Now we can substitute this expression for hhh into the area formula:

A=12bh=12b(csin⁡α)=12bcsin⁡α\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} A &=& \dfrac{1}{2}bh \\[1.5em] &=& \dfrac{1}{2}b(c \sin \alpha) \\[1.5em] &=& \dfrac{1}{2}bc \sin \alpha \end{array}A​===​21​bh21​b(csinα)21​bcsinα​

Similarly, we can arrive at the other two variations of the area formula by drawing the height from different vertices. This would give us A=12a(csin⁡β)A = \dfrac{1}{2}a(c\sin \beta)A=21​a(csinβ) and A=12a(bsin⁡γ)A = \dfrac{1}{2}a(b \sin \gamma)A=21​a(bsinγ).

So, the formula for the area of an oblique triangle is given by A=12absin⁡γ=12bcsin⁡α=12acsin⁡βA = \dfrac{1}{2}ab \sin \gamma = \dfrac{1}{2}bc \sin \alpha = \dfrac{1}{2}ac \sin \betaA=21​absinγ=21​bcsinα=21​acsinβ.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

Find the area of the triangle given β=42∘\beta = 42^\circβ=42∘, a=7.2 fta = 7.2 \text{ ft}a=7.2 ft, and c=3.4 ftc = 3.4 \text{ ft}c=3.4 ft. Round your answer to the nearest tenth.

Due to the fact we know the values of aaa, ccc, and β\betaβ, we can use the area formula A=12acsin⁡βA = \dfrac{1}{2}ac \sin \betaA=21​acsinβ to find the area of the triangle.

A=12acsin⁡β=12(7.2)(3.4)sin⁡42∘≈8.2\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} A &=& \dfrac{1}{2}ac \sin \beta \\[1.5em] &=& \dfrac{1}{2}(7.2)(3.4) \sin 42^\circ \\[1.5em] &\approx& 8.2 \end{array}A​==≈​21​acsinβ21​(7.2)(3.4)sin42∘8.2​

The area of the triangle is approximately 8.2 ft28.2 \text{ ft}^28.2 ft2.

Real World Applications​

The applications of the Law of Sines are countless especially in calculus, engineering, and physics involving three dimensions and motion.

Example‾\underline{Example}Example​

We can start by finding the value of angle CCC using the other two angles because all the angles in a triangle must sum to 180∘180^\circ180∘.

C=180∘−70∘−62∘=48∘C = 180^\circ - 70^\circ - 62^\circ = 48^\circC=180∘−70∘−62∘=48∘

sin⁡Aa=sin⁡Ccsin⁡70∘a=sin⁡48∘145asin⁡48∘=145sin⁡70∘a=145sin⁡70∘sin⁡48∘a≈183.35\begin{array}{ccccccccccccccccc} \dfrac{\sin A}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin C}{c} \\[1.5em] \dfrac{\sin 70^\circ}{a} &=& \dfrac{\sin 48^\circ}{145} \\[1.5em] a\sin 48^\circ &=& 145\sin 70^\circ \\[1.5em] a &=& \dfrac{145\sin 70^\circ}{\sin 48^\circ} \\[1.5em] a &\approx& 183.35 \end{array}asinA​asin70∘​asin48∘aa​====≈​csinC​145sin48∘​145sin70∘sin48∘145sin70∘​183.35​
